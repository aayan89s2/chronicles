Must Know

Core topics

  • Derivative rules, implicit differentiation, and related rates
  • Integrals, FTC, accumulation functions, and area/volume
  • Parametric, polar, and vector-valued motion
  • Sequences and series: convergence tests, Taylor, and Maclaurin

Speed review

Retest checklist

  • Know when to use geometric, p-series, ratio, root, and alternating tests
  • Check interval and radius of convergence carefully
  • Memorize common Taylor series for `e^x`, `sin x`, `cos x`, and `1 / (1 - x)`
  • State conclusions cleanly: converges, diverges, absolute, or conditional

Practice prompt

Series test warmup

Determine whether `sum from n=1 to infinity of (3^n / 4^n)` converges, and if it does, find the sum.

It converges because it is a geometric series with ratio `r = 3/4`, where `|r| < 1`. The sum is `(3/4) / (1 - 3/4) = 3`.

Tuesday FRQs

AP-style free-response set

FRQ 1

Toll Plaza Rates

Vehicles arrive at a toll plaza at rate `A(t) = 450 sqrt(sin(0.62t))` from 5 A.M. onward. Review the integral setup, average value, whether the rate is increasing at `t = 1`, and the maximum line length when arrivals exceed 400 vehicles per hour.

Set up totals with an integral of `A(t)` over the time interval, use average value as `(1 / (b - a)) integral from a to b of A(t) dt`, test increasing or decreasing with `A'(1)`, and analyze the line length with `N'(t) = A(t) - 400` so the biggest line happens when that expression changes from positive to negative.

FRQ 2

Particle Motion

A particle has `dx/dt = sqrt(1 + t^2)` and `dy/dt = ln(2 + t^2)` with position `(1, 5)` at `t = 4`. Review tangent slope, speed, acceleration, the `y`-coordinate at `t = 6`, and total distance traveled from `t = 4` to `t = 6`.

Use `dy/dx = (dy/dt) / (dx/dt)` for slope, speed as `sqrt((dx/dt)^2 + (dy/dt)^2)`, acceleration from the derivatives of `dx/dt` and `dy/dt`, the new `y`-coordinate from `y(6) = 5 + integral from 4 to 6 of dy/dt dt`, and total distance from the integral of speed over `[4, 6]`.

FRQ 3

Graph of f'

A differentiable function has `f(4) = 3`, and the graph of `f'` is built from a semicircle and two line segments on `0 <= x <= 7`. Review function values, inflection points, where `g(x) = f(x) - x` is decreasing, and the absolute minimum of `g`.

Recover `f` values with signed area under `f'`, find inflection points where `f'` switches from increasing to decreasing or vice versa, use `g'(x) = f'(x) - 1` to locate where `g` is decreasing, and compare endpoint and critical-point values to get the absolute minimum.

FRQ 4

Melting Ice Cone

A conical ice sculpture shrinks with radius `r(t)` and a table of `r'(t)` values. Review estimating `r''(8.5)`, using the Mean Value Theorem, a right Riemann sum for `integral of r'(t)`, and related rates for cone volume.

Approximate `r''(8.5)` with the average rate of change of `r'` from `t = 7` to `t = 10`, justify the existence of `r'(t) = -6` on `[0, 3]` with the Mean Value Theorem, build the right Riemann sum directly from the table widths and right endpoints, and differentiate `V = (1 / 3) pi r^2 h` with respect to time for the related rates part.

FRQ 5

Area and Volume

Work with region `R` under `y = 1/x` from `x = 1` to `x = 5`, plus the unbounded region `W` for `y = 1/x^2` to the right of `x = 3`. Review area, volume from cross sections, and volume of revolution for an improper integral.

The area is a basic definite integral of `1/x`, the solid with known cross sections uses a direct integral of the given rectangle area formula, and the volume of revolution for the unbounded region is an improper integral checked for convergence before evaluating.

FRQ 6

Power Series

The function `f(x) = x - x^3/3 + x^5/5 - x^7/7 + ...` appears as a power series. Review interval of convergence via ratio test, bounding a function value, differentiating the series, and evaluating `f'(1/6)`.

Apply the ratio test for the radius, then check endpoints separately. For the approximation bound, use the alternating series error idea. Differentiate term-by-term inside the interval of convergence, then plug in `x = 1/6` to evaluate the resulting geometric-style derivative series.
